How it works:

Parade of Trades is a simple simulation that helps us to understand the negative effects of variability in any system. In this simulation there is a "stockpile" of coins that need to move from their starting point above the process chain to their end point below the process chain. The simulation is over once all coins has been passed through the chain. The process chain consists of five steps, and each step takes turns in drawing coins from the step before it in the chain, or from the stockpile. To do this, each step takes turns to roll a dice, and can take the amount of coins from the step before it equal to the amount rolled, if there are enough coins available in that step. The simulation kicks off with the last step in the process chain (step 5 -> passed coin) indicated by the red light --- at the botom of the chain. Once the dice has been rolled, the dice is "passed" down the line to the next step in the chain (step 5) and that step's blue light --- is turned on. This step now rolls the dice, and takes that amount of coins from the step prior to it in the chain if they are available and so on. When the first step is reached, it draws from the stockpile. After each step in the chain has rolled, the "weeks" counter is incremented, indicated a "week" of work has passed. Additionally, each step has an inventory limit shows below the step. This is the maximum number of coins that the step can hold at any time, and is editable by the used.

-

The button runs all dice rolls until all coins are passed, and show the user the result of the simulation. The button resets the simulation

-

Additionally, the button allows the user to toggle between Variance and Stable mode. In Variance mode, the user sets a minimum and maximum limit for dice rolls (indicative of process spot capability) and the dice is rolled as explained above. In Stable mode, each dice roll is equal to 3.
